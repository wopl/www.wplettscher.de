SOA Enterprise Roadmap

Involvement

03/2007 - 05/2007

Customer

Automotive Breakdown Services

Role

IT-Architect / Strategy Consultant

To become even more flexible and responsive in the future our customer asked Capgemini to analyze and verify current business processes and the supporting IT-infrastructure for the vehicle breakdown organization and many supporting services.

Within a small team of consultants I analyzed the business requirements and their IT implications to propose different approaches of our customers future IT environment. In interactive workshops multiple szenarios have been worked out and evaluated with stakeholders to prepare a management recommendation.

The proposed solution is based on state of the art SOA technologies combined with traditional renewal of grown legacy systems to provide a smooth, uninterrupted transition without danger to disturb business operation of our customer.

Mobile Solution for Distribution Company

Involvement

07/2005 - 12/2005

Customer

Distribution Company (Subsidiary of Global Player)

Role

Lead IT-Architect

To improve quality of service and to gain more transparency for the process our customer is renewing it's parcel delivery. While today all data from pick-up until delivery has to be collected throughout the day, the new system will support online-connection between staff and backoffice via spezialized handheld-terminals and mobile GPRS-connections.
I initially was part of the proposal bid team and - as the responsible architect - held the the technical part of the final customer presentation to win this challenging, state of the art project. As the lead-architect I furthermore was responsible for top-level design of the whole application including

  • hendheld client-technologies
  • wireless GPRS connection
  • server architecture
  • database design
  • security design

For four months I guided a team of specialists until design of the application was approved by the customer. As of today (spring 2006) the project is under development and meanwhile in the successful delivery state..
Technologies: Sybase PowerDesigner, Oracle Database, Eclipse/Java for Server development, Microsoft C++ for Handheld development, GPRS mobile communication, WAP, HTTP(S) protocols, IBM MQ (Messaging), BEA Weblogic Application Server
