Automotive Diagnostic System

Involvement

12/2002 - 10/2003

Customer

European car manufacturer

Role

Lead IT-Architect

Since 2000 IBM has developed a new diagnostic approach for finding error-causes in complex systems. This approach has been applied with a European car manufacturer to his vehicles. A first major release was deployed in 2002 in Europe. To satisfy additional requirements a new release was planned to be implemented in 2003. I was assigned to lead the architecture team for this new version. While using the same - but enhanced - algorithm as in the previous version many new features have been introduced. Major enhancements included:

  • Optimization of communication (between components and to vehicle)
  • Enhancing Security issues and adapt it to customer’s needs
  • Enhancing Data Persistance issues
  • Ensuring further Scalability of the system
  • Adding National Language capabilities for worldwide deployment

During my involvement we finalized to gather requirements from the customer and based on this information the team built a Macro and Micro design. The realization (programming) was finished end of 2003. Since then the worldwide deployment has started.

Telematics Backend Architecture

Involvement

1/2002 - 10/2002

Customer

German car manufacturer

Role

Lead IT-Architect

Many car manufacturers in the last few years started to establish Telematics services for their vehicles. The discussion of introducing Telematics is mostly related to in-vehicle devices and technologies. To deploy state of the art Telematics services an IT backend infrstructure has to be established as well. Currently no standard concepts exist on the market.

In cooporation with a German car manufacturer the IBM team developed the architectural concept for a brand specific mobile portal for vehicles. It is based on innovative and open standard technologies. With the scalable and extendable outline the architecture supports different flows of data and processes also for future telematics services. The project especially focused on the following topics:

  • Communication between vehicle and backend
  • Master Data Management of users, vehicles, devices and services
  • Security issues like authentication, authorization, confidentiality and non-repudiation
  • Software Management and Distribution

Audi Telematics Architecture

Involvement

11/1999 - 12/2001

Customer

Audi, Ingolstadt

Role

Consulting Client IT-Architect

With help from IBM, Audi-Europe has created an innovative concept for upscale automobiles by integrating Internet access into its cars of the future. IBM has developed major components of the project’s wireless and Internet technology, adapted to the special requirements of automotive Internet communications. IBM and Motorola combined to deliver the telematics architecture and major components for the Audi A8 line introduced for sale in Europe.
I was engaged on-site during the initial design and early prototype phase in close cooporation with the customer’s EE chief designer.
